Experience

Global Alpha Researcher @ TrexQuant

Jan 2026 - Mar 2026


At Trexquant, I focused on quantitative finance by engineering dollar-neutral trading alphas that exploited pre-earnings market microstructure inefficiencies. My work involved forecasting time-dependent mean reversions by quantifying structural deviations between expected and realized variance. I also leveraged FinBERT embeddings to extract independent predictive signals from domain-specific text for anomaly detection. Alongside core research, I designed a hybrid BERT and statistical model for sequential text prediction, which secured a 67% win rate in the firm's internal machine learning challenge.

Data Science Intern @ Zepto

May 2025 - Jun 2025


At Zepto, I architected a Graph Neural Network (GNN) based recommender system designed to drive complementary product suggestions and boost user interaction. I modeled a massive heterogeneous graph—comprising 200K nodes and 31M edges—using product metadata (CLIP Embeddings) and historical co-purchase data. By designing a custom GNN encoder with L2-normalization and a dot-product decoder for link prediction, the system delivered substantial improvements in recommendation quality, notably driving a 42% lift in new-item Recall@10 and a 24% increase in overall Precision@10.

Research Intern @ IISc Bangalore

Jan 2025 - Jan 2026


Working under Professor Anirban Chakraborty at the Department of Computational and Data Sciences, I developed a zero-shot, unsupervised Video Anomaly Detection system that framed visual anomaly detection as a sophisticated language reasoning task. I engineered a multi-modal pipeline to extract temporal summaries from videos, creating high-quality, rich context inputs for LLMs. To overcome the limitations of classical analysis in detecting long-range temporal anomalies, I also designed and implemented a hierarchical context memory framework.

Research Intern @ IISc Bangalore

Dec 2023 - May 2024


In my initial tenure at IISc's CDS department, my research was heavily focused on optimizing Multi-task Learning (MTL) systems. I innovated a Gradient Normalization (GradNorm) algorithm incorporating task regularization, which successfully outperformed benchmark mean squared error (MSE) by 15%. After implementing this modified algorithm on real-world MTL problems to demonstrate its practical viability, I also contributed to research extending the application of GradNorm to complex NLP tasks, such as Relation Extraction and Named Entity Recognition.

Research Intern @ SJMSOM, IIT Bombay

I started off with a basic Song Recommendation system using K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN) and Mean Squared Error (MSE) to understand the basics of Recommender Systems and Embedding Space. Synthesizing Heartbeats

Master's Thesis using WGAN-GP. Developed a Conditional Transformer WGAN-GP pipeline to synthesize high-fidelity arrhythmia ECGs, enforcing physiological realism via an FFT based spectral loss and DTW-based rejection sampling. The architecture successfully improved downstream recall for minority classes under strict TSTR evaluation protocols.
